# 📦 Procurement & Product Fundamentals (D365FO)

## 🔍 Overview

This document explains the **core business and ERP concepts** behind:

- Vendors
- Products
- Procurement setup

These are **master data foundations** required before any purchasing activity.

---

# 🧾 Procurement in ERP

## 📌 What is Procurement?

Procurement is the process of:
> Purchasing goods or services from suppliers

---

## 🔁 Procurement Lifecycle

1. Vendor selection
2. Purchase order creation
3. Goods receipt
4. Invoice processing
5. Payment

---

## ✅ Key Concept

> Procurement success depends on **master data quality**

---

# 🏢 Vendors (Suppliers)

## 📌 What is a Vendor?

A vendor is:
> A business entity that provides goods or services

---

## 🧩 Vendor Master Data Includes:

- Name and address
- Payment terms
- Currency
- Tax information

---

## ✅ In D365FO

Vendor records are used in:
- Purchase orders
- Vendor invoices
- Payments

---

# 📦 Products in D365FO

## 📌 What is a Product?

A product represents:
> Something the company buys, sells, or stores

---

## 🔁 Product Lifecycle in D365FO

### Step 1 – Product Definition (Global)
- Created once
- Shared across companies

### Step 2 – Released Product (Company-specific)
- Activated in a legal entity
- Used in operations

---

## ✅ Key Difference

| Type | Scope |
|------|------|
| Product | Global |
| Released Product | Legal entity |

---

# 🏷️ Product Types

- Item → Physical goods (inventory tracked)
- Service → Non-physical (no inventory)

---

# 📊 Procurement Categories

## 📌 What are Procurement Categories?

Categories are used to:
- Classify purchases
- Control spending
- Support reporting

---

## ✅ Examples

- Office Supplies
- IT Equipment
- Services

---

# 🔐 Procurement Controls (Conceptual)

Organizations may define:
- Approval thresholds
- Purchase policies
- Vendor restrictions

---

# ⚠️ Common Mistakes

- Poor product naming conventions
- Duplicate vendor records
- No category classification
- Inconsistent master data

---

# ✅ Key Takeaways

- Procurement starts with master data
- Vendors and products must be defined before transactions
- Product lifecycle matters (global vs released)
- Good data = good ERP process

---
